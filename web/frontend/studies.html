Ablation Study: Methodology Impact

Focus: Preprocessing & Split Status: Concluded

1. Bandpass Filtering (1-40Hz)

Raw EEG signals are contaminated by various noise sources, including power line interference (60Hz/50Hz) and EMG artifacts (muscle movement). We applied a Band-Pass Filter (1-40Hz) to isolate the relevant brainwave bands: Delta, Theta, Alpha, and Beta. This range is critical for cognitive load and fatigue assessment.

2. Subject Isolation vs. Random Split

To validate robustness, we compared two data splitting strategies. Random Split shuffles all windows, allowing the model to see chunks of the same patient in both train and test sets (potential Data Leakage). Subject Isolation ensures that the test set consists entirely of unseen patients.

Fig 1. Accuracy comparison between methodologies.

  • Scenario A (Subject Isolation): 92.44% Accuracy (Loss: 0.2122). Represents real-world performance.
  • Scenario B (Random Split): 89.06% Accuracy (Loss: 0.2752).
Interpretation: Surprisingly, the model performed better in the isolated scenario. This suggests the architecture has achieved high Identity Invariance, focusing on the universal neural signature of Burnout rather than idiosyncratic patient features.

Benchmark: SVM vs. Few-Shot

Baseline: Linear SVM Proposed: Prototypical Net

We compared our Deep Learning approach against a classic Machine Learning baseline: Support Vector Machine (SVM) with a Linear Kernel. Both models were trained and tested under strict Subject Isolation.

Performance Gap

Metric Classic SVM Few-Shot (Proposed) Improvement
Accuracy 75.95% 89.40% +13.45%
F1-Score 0.76 0.89 +0.13

Analysis

The SVM Linear kernel struggles to capture the complex, non-linear relationships in EEG data across different subjects. It attempts to draw a straight hyperplane to separate classes, which fails when inter-subject variability is high.

In contrast, our Prototypical Network learns a metric space where semantic similarity (Burnout vs. Relaxed) is preserved, regardless of the individual patient's baseline brain activity. This results in superior generalization.

Confusion Matrix Analysis

Test Set: 20% Unseen Subjects

Quadrant Breakdown

The matrix reveals the model's behavior on the test set. Key observations:

  • True Positives (Burnout): High detection rate (47.96%). The model rarely misses a severe burnout case.
  • False Positives: The SVM baseline generated 113 False Positives, misclassifying relaxed subjects as Burnout. Our Few-Shot model significantly reduced this noise.
Conclusion: The model maintains a balanced tradeoff between Sensitivity and Specificity, making it suitable for clinical screening support.
